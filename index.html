Forget marathons, there is nothing harder than ultrarunning and these 10 athletes are some of the best in the world in the discipline of long distance.

Being an ultrarunner takes hard work, passion, and natural talent. These races are longer than a marathon, generally run on trails, and typically range in length from 50 (80 km) to 100 miles (160 km). Famous events include the Western States 100, the Hardrock 100, and the Ultra-Trail du Mont-Blanc.

How does a runner go from being in the peloton to getting on the podium frequently? “There comes a point where doing kilometers and kilometers is not going to be enough to really progress. You need to train smart, rest well and above all have a passion for hitting the slopes, ”says British ultrarunner Damian Hall.

As in any elite sport, there is a circuit specialized in ultrarunning and the best athletes usually coincide in the most important events. Despite the fact that these athletes are very competitive, the atmosphere is often one of friendly rivalry

“I don't think I've ever met a trail ultrarunner that I didn't like. Camaraderie is more important than competition. People help you when you have problems ”.

There is no definitive list but here are 10 of the best, of which six will participate this year in the now classic Ultra-Trail du Mont-Blanc.

1.Ryan Sanders

Birthday: March 10,1982
Nationality: South African
Instagram: @Ryansandes

His first success in a classic test was on the Leadville 100 in 2011, when he reached the third fastest time in his first 100-mile race. He became the first athlete to win on all continents, his victories including the Western States 100, the TransGranCanaria (twice) and the Patagonian International Marathon.

“If you love what you do, it is easy to be successful. I like to set goals and not stop until I reach them. I imagine you could say that I am a stubborn. I don't stop until I have achieved my goal ”.

2.Killian Jornet

Birthday: October 27,1987
Nationality: Spanish
Instagram: @Kilianjornet

For the Catalan an ultrarunning test is a mere warm-up. He has won the major trail races and also has the best ascents and descents of the Matterhorn, Mont Blanc, Denali and Everest.

The son of a mountain guide, he grew up in a cabin 2,000 meters high. Before taking on bigger trails, he was already a world champion in sky running. His list of ultrarunning victories includes three UTMBs, four Hardrock 100s and a Western States 100. In addition, he has the fastest time at the famous English Bob Graham Round.

“I like being able to be in the mountains every day. I like doing long and short runs, vertical kilometers, sky running, ascents, crossing mountain ranges… Each experience is a different enrichment ”.

3.Pau Capell

Birthday: September 10,1991
Nationality: Spanish
Instagram: @PauCapell

Pau Capell has been at the top of the Ultra Trail World Tour for the past two years and took a landslide victory at the 2019 UTMB.

He started running in 2013 to recover from a soccer injury. Only one year later he won the Ultra Sierra Nevada and the Ultra Trail Mallorca, since then he has also triumphed in the Ultra Trail Australia, the Eiger Ultra, the Patagonia Run, the Mozart 100 and in the last three races of the TransGranCanaria.

“Every race is different, so the strategy is different in every race. My motivation is always the same: enjoy when I train and do the best I can in the competition. You have to enjoy yourself to be able to reach 100 percent ”.

4.Jim Walmsley

Birthday: January 17,1990
Nationality: American
Instagram: @walmsleyruns

He has won the UltraRunner of the Year title four times, in addition to achieving victory in the last two races of the Western States 100. On the other hand, he holds the unofficial world record for the 50-mile (80 km) race.

Walmsley learned to run at the military academy. He won three JFK 50s, two Lake Sonoma 50s, the Tawawera Ultra and the Gorge Falls 100k, then set the record two years in a row at the Western States. He set the fastest time at the Grand Canyon Rim-to-Rim in 2016, which was declared "one of the best records ever."

“You have to dream big. Otherwise you will not think that you are capable of achieving those goals, so you will never do it. It doesn't matter if you talk about it or not, the important thing is that you have to believe in yourself ”.

5.Xavier Thevenard

Birthday: Birthday:March 6,1988
Nationality: French
Instagram: @xaviertenard

Thevenard was the only person to have won the Ultra-Trail du Mont-Blanc Grand Slam by achieving victory in all four categories, including the three main races.

In 2010 he won the 99 km CCC first time. Three years later he triumphed in the 170 km UTMB, then took the victory again in 2015 and 2018, in addition to finishing second in 2019. Other triumphs include the 119 km CCC (2014), the 55 km CCC (2016) and the Mt Fuji Ultra (2019).

“In ultra running events the fundamental values ​​are the ability to manage and patience. No matter the problem, the conditions or the surprises, the key is to stay calm and focused. Patience is everything ”.
